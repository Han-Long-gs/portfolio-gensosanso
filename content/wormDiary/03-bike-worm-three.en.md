I am a worm who rides a bicycle. I lick the air of this season. Chlorophyll dances in my mouth. Desire dances in my mouth, like the heat of a pot left on a burning stove for three minutes. In theory I have no mouth, yet I seem to possess the faculty of imagination. My experience is not grounded in common sense but in fantasy, so today I ride my bicycle backward. My illusions tell me this way I can return to the past. I have never truly been persuaded by the claim that the past was better. My longing to ***go back*** comes more from fear of the unknown. Thinking this, I ride to Negative 41st Street. A clean sheet of paper lies at the curb, covered in neat black handwriting. I recognize it—it is my rear wheel, the one that never disappeared.
