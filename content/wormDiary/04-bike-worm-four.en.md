I am a worm who rides a bicycle. I seem to ride only when the weather is not too hot. The unfamiliar feels so familiar; the familiar feels so unfamiliar. I dislike this sensation, and the heat of summer evaporates it. Losing the five senses troubles me more than any vivid disgust—a sticky discomfort all over. Many people have asked how I manage to ride a bicycle. I do not really know. I simply ride, using my small mouthparts, toward a place I cannot name.
