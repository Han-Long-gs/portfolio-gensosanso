I am a worm who rides a bicycle. The sunlight is generous. I watch wasps mating, ants lining up to carry fragments of seaweed, placing them on my fifth segment. A cherry blossom petal lands on my seventeenth. At night the searchlights are blinding. Beside a garbage truck I see a friend who looks very much like me—red, green, blue, purple, transparent bodies. I nudge it. It never responds. We pass two silent hours together. Later I grow too tired, so I coil myself around the handlebar, longing for an eternal sleep.
