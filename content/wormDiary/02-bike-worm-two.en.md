I am a worm who rides a bicycle. I pass the beach and arrange broken shells into a crooked row. I lie on top of them and imagine that I have bones. My spirit inhabits a skeleton that does not exist; it is an apparition that has never belonged to my life. I think I understand why humans love invented stories. The false becomes real, the nonexistent becomes existent. Something as crooked as I am, resting on paper, can turn illusion into something almost touchable. It feels miraculous. I want to try. On 41st Street I find a discarded sheet of paper. I crawl across it, leaving a wet trace that looks like the front wheel I lost last year. I still miss my front wheel very much. I imagine someone will touch this page, and my front wheel will become real again, instead of remaining a tedious dream.
